Growing Flowers in Florida
Picture Name Colors Blooming Season When to Plant How to Plant
Jacobina
Pink or White
Year Round
Summer-Winter

  • When planting the seed, keep rootballs at or slightly above the soil line.

  • Mix soil with organic matter, put holes in soil.

  • Grow in shaded dry area and water weekly to keep soil moist.
Bromeliad
Pink, Red, Orange, and Yellow
Year Round
Summer-Winter

  • When planting a Bromeliad pup plant in a bed of mulch and shade.

  • Keep Bromeliad pups fairly filled with water and fertilize every two weeks.

  • You can also use orchid mix, peat, perlite, and bark.
Periwinkle
Pink, White, and Rose
Year Round
Spring-Summer

  • Plant in dry or sandy soil.

  • You can plant cuttings in a 50/50 mix of peat moss and perlite and water regularly.

  • It is best to plant around the rainy season.
Beach Sunflower
Bright Yellow
Year Round
Fall-Winter

  • Can be started from either seeds or cuttings.

  • Plant in sandy organic, well drained soils.

  • Receives nutrients from decomposing leaves, rainwater, and fertilizer.
Calla Lily
White, Pink, yellow, and purple
Spring
Fall-Winter

  • Grows best in 6-8 hours of sunlight.

  • Provide adequate water to keep soil moist.

  • Plant in compost, peat moss, and organic matter.
Snapdragon
Red, Yellow, Pink, Orange
Fall-Spring
Fall-Spring

  • Put under a sunny area.

  • Frequently water and keep soil moist

  • As Snapdragon grows, use spikes or garden wire for it to climb on.
Marigold
Yellow, Orange, Red, Maroon, and White
Fall-Spring
Spring-Fall

  • Can be planted in full sun.

  • Use a mix of organic and sandy soil and keep frequently watered.

  • Use fertilizer every 3-4 weeks
Pansy
Purple, Blue, Orange, White, Yellow, Pink, and Red
Fall-Winter
Fall

  • Use organic and sandy soil, and keep moist

  • Plant seeds with a fair amount of space, around 6-8 inches apart.

  • Use fertilizer every 3-4 weeks.
